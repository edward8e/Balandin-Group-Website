Plenary, Keynote and Invited Talks NDL & POEM

  • Plenary Lecture, “Properties and applications of graphene,” IEEE NANO – 11th International Conference on Nanotechnology, Portland, Oregon, USA, 2011 – Professor Balandin gave this plenary lecture at the flagship IEEE nanotechnology conference after receiving the IEEE Pioneer Award in Nanotechnology for his work on nanoscale phonon engineering.
  • Plenary Lecture, “Phonons in graphene and van der Waals materials” Materials Research Society (MRS) Fall Meeting, Boston, USA, 2013 - Professor Balandin gave this plenary lecture at the MRS Fall meeting after receiving The MRS Medal for discovery of the unique heat conduction properties of graphene.
  • Keynote Talk, “Low-frequency current fluctuations and 1/f noise in graphene,” Graphene Week, Gothenburg, Sweden, 2014
  • Invited Talk “Properties and device applications of two-dimensional charge density wave materials”, Materials Research Society (MRS) Spring Meeting, Phoenix, Arizona, USA, 2017
  • Keynote Opening Talk, “Phononics in low-dimensional materials,” International CECAM Workshop Nanophononics, University of Bremen, Germany, 2013
  • Invited Talk “Transition from quasi-2D to quasi-1D van der Waals materials,” Materials Research Society (MRS) Spring Meeting, Phoenix, Arizona, USA, 2017
